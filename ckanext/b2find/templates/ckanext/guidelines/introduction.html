    Welcome to the guidelines of the metadata service EUDAT-B2FIND for data providers. These guidelines are intended to provide information about the requirements for successful integration in B2FIND.

    Intended Audience

    The target readership for this document are research data providers and repository managers who intend to publish their metadata in EUDAT-B2FIND.

    Objective

    EUDAT-B2FIND gathers together diverse metadata related to research output of many heterogeneous sources, with the aim of providing a unified discovery portal allowing widespread and cross-disciplinary search and access to the underlying data collections.

    The B2FIND guidelines provide instructions to be followed and policies to be fulfilled during the establishment of the ingestion workflow associated with successful publishing of metadata.

    Our intention is to provide a low-barrier approach for integrating research data related metadata to B2FIND.

    Open research data principles

    EUDAT propagates 'best practices' which follow the so called FAIR principles. We refer here to the paper Guidelines on FAIR Data Management in Horizon 2020 . Amongst other principles, the concordat promotes

    • the availability of data supporting scholarly publications,
    • the use of data repositories,
    • the value of data curation to enable data access and reuse,
    • support for developing researchers’ data skills,
    • cultural norms of academia that ensure individuals can gain credit for data sharing.

    To the extent that these principles affect metadata we address them in the following sections.

    Contents of the Guidelines

    The guidelines are divided in three thematic paragraphs :

    1. Providing Metadata for EUDAT-B2FIND establishes policies and directives, e.g.about which syntax, structure and licencing of the provided metadata is required.
    2. Harvesting of Metadata by B2FIND describes the interfaces and APIs supported by B2FIND to transfer metadata exposed by the data providers to B2FIND.
    3. Mapping onto the common B2FIND Schema explains how the existing, community-specific metadata are homogenised to the unique and common B2FIND schema.
